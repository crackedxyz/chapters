1. Revolutions that Influenced the World

  • Explain the major causes of the American Revolution.
  • What were the results of the French Revolution?
  • Describe the impact of the Russian Revolution on the world.
  • Compare the American and French Revolutions in terms of their objectives and outcomes.
  • How did the Chinese Revolution influence the world?

2. British Exploitation and Resistance

  • What were the main causes and results of the 1857 Revolt?
  • Explain the role of Mahatma Gandhi in the Indian freedom struggle.
  • Describe the Non-Cooperation Movement and its impact.
  • What was the Doctrine of Lapse? How did it affect Indian rulers?
  • Explain the significance of the Quit India Movement (1942).

3. Kerala Towards Modernity

  • Write a short note on Sree Narayana Guru and his social reforms.
  • What was the Malabar Rebellion (1921)? State its causes.
  • Explain the importance of the Temple Entry Proclamation (1936).
  • Discuss the contributions of Ayyankali to social reforms.
  • What were the aims of the Vaikom Satyagraha?

4. Struggle and Freedom

  • What were the major reasons for the Partition of Bengal (1905)?
  • Explain the objectives of the Civil Disobedience Movement.
  • Describe the contributions of Subhas Chandra Bose and the INA.
  • What was the Rowlatt Act? Why was it opposed by Indians?
  • Discuss the role of women in the Indian freedom struggle.

5. Culture and Nationalism

  • How did language and literature contribute to Indian nationalism?
  • Explain the role of newspapers in the freedom struggle.
  • What was the significance of the Swadeshi Movement?
  • How did art and music inspire the Indian freedom movement?
  • Discuss the contribution of Rabindranath Tagore to cultural nationalism.

6. Civic Consciousness

  • What are the Fundamental Rights in the Indian Constitution?
  • Explain the duties of a responsible citizen.
  • How does civic consciousness help in building a better society?
  • Describe the role of Panchayati Raj in local governance.
  • Why is voting important in a democracy?

7. Sociology: What? Why?

  • Define Sociology. Why is it important?
  • Explain the methods of sociological study (Survey, Observation, Case Study).
  • What are social institutions? Give examples.
  • Who is called the Father of Sociology? State his contributions.
  • Differentiate between rural sociology and urban sociology.

8. Public Administration

  • What is Public Administration? Explain its importance.
  • Define bureaucracy. What are its main features?
  • Explain the structure of Indian Administrative Services (IAS).
  • What are the main functions of local self-government?
  • Describe the difference between public and private administration.
